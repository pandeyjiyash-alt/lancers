About Michael

Michael holds a PhD in Machine Learning from Stanford University, where his research focused on efficient transformer architectures for low-resource NLP tasks. He has published 8 peer-reviewed papers in venues including NeurIPS, ICML, and ACL.

After completing his PhD, Michael spent four years at a leading AI research lab before joining Vantade to apply cutting-edge research to real business problems. He has shipped AI features to products with over 5 million active users, and has a particular talent for translating complex model capabilities into practical, maintainable engineering.

His current focus is helping clients integrate large language models — GPT-4o, Claude, and open-source alternatives like Llama — into their products through retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), fine-tuning, and structured output pipelines. He firmly believes that the best AI system is the one that solves the problem reliably, not the one with the most parameters.
