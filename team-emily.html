About Emily

Emily started her career as a graphic designer before discovering a passion for interaction design — the point where visual communication meets human psychology. She spent five years at a leading UX agency in San Francisco, where she led redesigns for Fortune 500 clients and won two UX awards from the Interaction Design Association.

Her design philosophy is simple: every decision should be traceable back to a user need or a business metric. She runs discovery workshops, conducts usability tests, and reviews analytics data before touching any design tool. When she does open Figma, it's with a clear hypothesis about what will move the needle.

At Vantade, Emily is responsible for making sure every product the team ships is not only beautiful but genuinely usable. She has introduced an internal design system that has cut front-end development time by 35% and maintains a library of reusable, accessible components used across all client projects.
